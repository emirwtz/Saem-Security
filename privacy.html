Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

1. Who we are

SAEM Security ("we", "us") is a cybersecurity company operating from Istanbul, Turkey and Damascus, Syria. This policy explains what data we collect when you visit saemsecurity.com, why we collect it, and how you can reach us about it. Contact: info@saemsecurity.com.

2. Data we collect automatically

Every time you load a page on this site, a lightweight visit record is created and stored in our Firebase (Google Cloud) database. It contains:

  • IP address and the approximate location it resolves to (country/city)
  • Page URL visited and the time of the visit
  • Browser/device details: screen resolution and user-agent string

This is used solely for basic traffic analytics and to help us detect abuse of our systems. We do not sell this data or use it for advertising, and only one record is stored per browsing session.

3. Data stored in your browser

We use localStorage to remember your language and dark/light mode preference between visits. This stays on your device and is never sent to our servers.

4. Data you submit directly

If you use our contact form, the name, email, subject, and message you enter are sent to our inbox via Formspree so we can respond to you. We keep this correspondence only as long as needed to handle your request.

5. Third-party services we use

  • Google Firebase / Firestore — stores visit analytics
  • ipwho.is — resolves your IP to an approximate country/city, and to serve you the right language automatically
  • Formspree — delivers contact-form submissions to us
  • Google Fonts and Cloudflare — deliver fonts and site infrastructure

Each of these providers may process your IP address as part of delivering their service to us.

6. Your rights

You can ask us what data we hold about you, request its deletion, or object to its processing at any time by writing to info@saemsecurity.com. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this page as our practices evolve. The "last updated" date above reflects the most recent revision.
